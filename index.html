Understanding Open Educational Resources

OER are teaching, learning, and research materials intentionally created and licensed to be free for anyone to use, modify, and share.

Free Access

OER materials are available at no cost, removing financial barriers to education. Anyone can access, download, and use these resources without subscription fees or paywalls.

Open Licensing

Creative Commons licenses allow creators to specify how others can use their work. Users can retain, reuse, revise, remix, and redistribute content legally.

Community Driven

OER thrives on collaboration. Educators worldwide contribute, improve, and adapt resources to fit diverse cultural and educational contexts.

Open Licenses & Creative Commons

Understanding licenses is crucial for legally using, sharing, and creating OER. Learn how to apply these licenses to your own work.

Creative Commons License Types

CC BY (Attribution)

Most permissive license. Allows others to distribute, remix, adapt, and build upon your work, even commercially, as long as they credit you.

CC BY-SA (ShareAlike)

Allows remixing, adapting, and building upon your work even for commercial purposes, as long as they license their new creations under identical terms (like Wikipedia).

CC BY-NC (NonCommercial)

Others can remix, adapt, and build upon your work non-commercially. Their new works must also acknowledge you and be non-commercial.

CC BY-ND (NoDerivatives)

Allows for redistribution, commercial and non-commercial, as long as it is passed along unchanged and in whole, with credit to you.

CC0 (Public Domain Dedication)

You waive all copyright and related rights, placing the work as completely as possible in the public domain. Others can freely build upon, enhance and reuse the work.

How to Apply a CC License

1

Choose Your License

Visit creativecommons.org/choose and answer questions about how you want others to use your work. The tool will recommend the best license.

2

Get the HTML Code

Copy the generated HTML code or license icons. Include this on your website, document footer, or resource landing page.

3

Mark Your Content

Include the license notice on every page/resource. Format: "This work is licensed under a Creative Commons [License Name] 4.0 International License."

4

Attribute Properly

When using others' OER, always provide proper attribution: Title, Author, Source, License (TASL method).

Pro Tip for Educators

Always check the license before remixing content. CC BY-SA requires you to share alike, while CC BY-NC cannot be used in commercial courses without permission.
